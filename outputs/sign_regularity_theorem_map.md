## Candidate Theorem Ecosystems

## 1. PF-Infinity / Toeplitz Total Positivity

### Classical Shape

For a one-sided sequence, the PF-infinity route studies total positivity of the Toeplitz matrix generated by the sequence. The Aissen-Schoenberg-Whitney/Edrei theorem characterizes generating functions of totally positive sequences and is the classical bridge to real-negative zeros.

Relevant source anchors:

- Aissen, Schoenberg, Whitney, "On the generating functions of totally positive sequences I", listed in the references of Belton-Guillot-Khare-Putinar.
- Edrei's companion theorem is also listed there.
- Belton-Guillot-Khare-Putinar review modern preservers for totally positive kernels and Polya frequency functions.

### Fit

This theorem family is directly connected to real-rootedness and Laguerre-Polya type conclusions.

### Normalization Correction

The bundle reports robust negative Toeplitz minors for `A_k(lambda)`. That statement has now been reproduced for the upper-triangular Toeplitz matrix generated by the exponential/Jensen coefficients `A_k`.

However, the ASW/Edrei coefficient theorem for the restricted Laguerre-Polya class is formulated for ordinary Taylor coefficients. In our notation:

```text
F_lambda(s) = sum A_k(lambda) s^k / k!
c_k(lambda) = A_k(lambda) / k!
```

Therefore the simple PF-infinity coefficient route should test `c_k`, not `A_k`.

## 2. Schoenberg Totally Positive Functions

### Classical Shape

Schoenberg's theory studies totally positive functions and their transforms. Groechenig's paper explicitly reviews Schoenberg's characterization and its connection to the Laguerre-Polya class, and states that this gives an RH-equivalent condition.

Source:

- Karlheinz Groechenig, "Schoenberg's Theory of Totally Positive Functions and the Riemann Zeta Function", arXiv:2007.12889.

### Fit

This is highly relevant because it is already framed around zeta/RH and total positivity, and it connects total positivity to Laguerre-Polya.

### Misfit

It may simply repackage an RH-equivalent condition rather than giving a new accessible bridge. We must identify whether our signed Hankel evidence corresponds to the actual Schoenberg condition or merely another equivalent formulation.

A further warning comes from a 2026 arXiv preprint claiming certified PF5 failure for the direct de Bruijn-Newman kernel. That preprint concerns translation-kernel Toeplitz minors of the heat kernel itself, not the ordinary coefficient Toeplitz minors of `c_k = A_k/k!`. So it does not kill the coefficient PF route, but it makes any direct-kernel PF-infinity route high risk unless the claimed obstruction is resolved.

### Verdict

Priority reading target.

Concrete task:

Extract Groechenig's RH-equivalent condition and compare it line-by-line against:

```text
Phi_lambda translation minors
A_k signed Hankel minors
Jensen polynomial hyperbolicity
```

## 3. Jensen Polynomial Hyperbolicity

### Classical Shape

Polya proved RH is equivalent to hyperbolicity of the relevant Jensen polynomials. Griffin-Ono-Rolen-Zagier prove broad asymptotic hyperbolicity results and hyperbolicity for low degrees.

Source:

- Griffin, Ono, Rolen, Zagier, arXiv:1902.07321.

### Fit

Our finite Sturm tests are exactly Jensen-polynomial tests.

### Misfit

Farmer argues that Jensen-polynomial asymptotics are not a plausible route to RH by themselves. More finite or asymptotic Jensen pass counts will not be enough unless we introduce a genuinely global mechanism.

Source:

- David W. Farmer, arXiv:2008.07206.

### Verdict

Use Jensen tests as diagnostics and consequences, not as the bridge by themselves.

Concrete requirement:

Find a theorem proving all-degree/all-shift Jensen hyperbolicity from a stronger structural condition, not just more finite tests.

## 4. Hankel Sign-Regularity / k-Sign Consistency

### Shape

Recent work on Hankel matrices shows that verifying sign consistency of all kth-order minors can sometimes be reduced to checking structured/consecutive minors.

Source:

- Grussler-Damm, "Efficient k-Sign Consistency Verification of Hankel Matrices via Schur Polynomials", arXiv:2602.08122.

### Fit

Our evidence currently checks consecutive principal shifted Hankel minors:

```text
det(A_{i+j+s})_{i,j=0}^m.
```

The paper is useful for upgrading the finite test design because it rewrites
k-sign consistency of Hankel matrices in terms of structured minors of an
associated reshaped Hankel matrix.

### Misfit

The current certified grid checks shifted-principal minors only. The
Grussler-Damm reduction is not the same condition, and the paper explicitly
does not collapse the problem to checking only ordinary consecutive k-minors.
It is also a verification/combinatorial reduction theorem; it does not by
itself imply real-rootedness, Laguerre-Polya membership, or `Lambda <= 0`.

### Verdict

Useful for strengthening the sign-regularity hypothesis and designing the
next finite checks, not sufficient as the RH bridge.

Concrete task:

Extend the current Arb/enclosure-backed reshaped-minor certificate frontier
for `A_k(lambda)` beyond `k = 2..5`, `N = 18`, identify the matching
all-order sign convention, and keep shifted-principal minors classified as
evidence rather than full k-sign consistency until the required reduction and
all-order theorem are proved.

## 5. Indefinite Moment Problems / Hankel Determinant Problem

### Shape

Classical moment problems use positive semidefinite Hankel matrices. Indefinite analogues and Hankel determinant problems study sign patterns and determinant sequences without positivity.

Source:

- Bakan-Berg, "Solvability of the Hankel determinant problem for real sequences", arXiv:1605.01196.

### Fit

Our determinants are not ordinary positive Hankel determinants. Their alternating signature suggests an indefinite moment or signed functional interpretation.

### Misfit

Indefinite Hankel determinant solvability does not automatically give real-rooted Jensen polynomials or LP membership.

### Verdict

Useful language for interpreting the signed determinants. Probably not the direct bridge unless paired with a spectral/continued-fraction theorem.

## 6. Continued Fractions / J-Fractions

### Shape

Hankel determinant signs often encode coefficients of continued fractions, orthogonal polynomial recurrences, or moment functionals.

### Fit

The sign pattern

```text
+, -, -, +, +, -, -, +, ...
```

by determinant order suggests a hidden transformation to a positive recurrence after a rotation or variable substitution.

### Misfit

Checkpoint 16 reports that the ordinary orthogonal-polynomial/interlacing route failed. Any continued-fraction interpretation must be nonstandard or signed.

### Verdict

Promising as a structural explanation, but it must explain why ordinary interlacing fails while Jensen hyperbolicity survives.

## Working Hypothesis Refined

The current best hypothesis is not:

```text
A_k is PF-infinity.
```

There are now two active, distinct hypotheses:

```text
1. The exponential/Jensen sequence A_k generates an indefinite/signed
   Hankel structure that becomes useful only after a conjugation,
   rotation, transform, or kernel-level reinterpretation.

2. The ordinary Taylor sequence c_k = A_k/k! may satisfy the classical
   PF-infinity Toeplitz condition relevant to the restricted
   Laguerre-Polya theorem.
```
