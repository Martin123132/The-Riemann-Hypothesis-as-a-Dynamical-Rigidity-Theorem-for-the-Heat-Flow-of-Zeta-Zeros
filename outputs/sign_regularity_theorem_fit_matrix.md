Status: theorem-search artifact. This is not a proof of PF-infinity, Laguerre-Polya membership, RH, or `Lambda <= 0`.

## Purpose

The current evidence contains three different positivity shadows:

```text
1. signed Hankel determinants for A_k(lambda)
2. Toeplitz/PF minors for c_k(lambda) = A_k(lambda)/k!
3. Edrei-log signs and power-Hankel diagnostics for d_k(lambda)=c_k/c_0
```

This file compares them against known total-positivity and Laguerre-Polya theorem families. The goal is to identify which theorem could actually close the bridge and which theorem only sounds adjacent.

## Fit/Misfit Matrix

### 1. ASW/Edrei One-Sided PF-Infinity

The theorem family characterizes one-sided PF-infinity sequences by the generating functions of their coefficient sequences.

Fit:

```text
Excellent for c_k = A_k/k!.
If c_k(0) is PF-infinity, ASW/Edrei gives the desired restricted
Laguerre-Polya conclusion for F_0.
```

Misfit:

```text
It does not provide a way to prove c_k is PF-infinity.
It is an endpoint/classification theorem.
Finite minors do not activate it.
Signed-Hankel signs of A_k do not activate it.
```

Referee-safe statement:

```text
ASW/Edrei identifies the exact all-order condition needed:
the Toeplitz matrix generated by c_k(0) must be totally nonnegative.
```

Forbidden statement:

```text
The finite Toeplitz certificate ledger proves ASW/Edrei hypotheses.
```

### 3. Schoenberg PF Functions

Schoenberg's theory relates totally positive functions to reciprocal Laplace transforms of Laguerre-Polya functions. Groechenig records an RH-equivalent condition in this language.

Fit:

```text
Strong conceptual fit with the Newman/RH endpoint.
Good source of language for total positivity, reciprocal transforms,
and Laguerre-Polya closure.
```

Misfit:

```text
It concerns PF functions and reciprocal transform data, not directly the
finite signed-Hankel determinants of A_k nor the coefficient Toeplitz
minors of c_k.
```

Important separation:

```text
direct kernel PF function
  != coefficient PF sequence c_k
  != signed Hankel sequence A_k
  != Schoenberg reciprocal-transform condition
```

Referee-safe use:

```text
Use Schoenberg/Groechenig as an endpoint-equivalence and warning map,
not as a proof of the current coefficient positivity claims.
```

### 4. Jensen Polynomial Criterion

Polya's Jensen criterion says RH is equivalent to hyperbolicity of all relevant Jensen polynomials. Griffin-Ono-Rolen-Zagier prove broad asymptotic hyperbolicity results; Farmer explains why such asymptotic Jensen routes do not by themselves give a plausible RH proof.

Fit:

```text
Our Jensen/Sturm finite tests are testing the right kind of object.
They are excellent falsification and normalization checks.
```

Misfit:

```text
The criterion needs all degrees and shifts.
Asymptotic fixed-degree hyperbolicity and finite Sturm grids do not close
the Newman direction.
```

Referee-safe statement:

```text
Jensen tests are necessary finite diagnostics and can catch bad normalizations.
```

Forbidden statement:

```text
Many finite or asymptotic Jensen passes constitute a route to RH.
```

### 5. Hankel Total Positivity / Stieltjes Moment Theory


```text

reconstructing the product requires an all-n representation and analytic
```

### 6. Hankel Sign-Consistency Reductions

of Hankel matrices via associated Schur polynomials. In the paper's notation,
the reduction checks row-consecutive minors of a reshaped Hankel matrix built
from the original Hankel data. The authors also caution that this does not
simplify further to checking only ordinary consecutive k-minors.

Fit:

```text
Useful for upgrading the design of finite signed-Hankel tests.
It gives a candidate finite verification shape for k-sign consistency,
provided we test the right reshaped minors rather than only principal shifts.
```

```text

determinants det(A_{i+j+s})_{i,j=0}^m.
That is not the same as the full Grussler-Damm reshaped-minor condition.
The theorem is a sign-consistency verification tool, not a theorem from
```

### 7. Multiplier/Closure Theorems

The sequence:

```text
b_k = 1/(2k)!
sum b_k z^k = cosh(sqrt(z))
```

is compatible with a restricted Laguerre-Polya object.

Fit:

```text
This explains why c_k = mu_{2k}/(2k)! is a better candidate than A_k.
```

Misfit:

```text
Multiplying a generic moment sequence mu_{2k} by b_k does not automatically
preserve or create PF-infinity. Stieltjes/Hankel positivity of moments is
not Toeplitz total positivity.
```

Exact countermodel gate:

```text
python work/rh_compute/scripts/countermodel_gate_examples.py
```

The positive measure `10 delta_0 + delta_1 + delta_2` has
Stieltjes/Hankel-nonnegative moments, with leading Hankel determinants
`12, 51, 40, 0`. But after `c_k = mu_k/(2k)!`, the order-2 Toeplitz/PF minor
is:

```text
c_1^2 - c_0 c_2 = -1/4.
```

Therefore any multiplier/closure theorem used here must verify hypotheses
stronger than ordinary moment positivity.

Required upgrade:

```text
a named closure theorem whose exact hypotheses are verified for mu_{2k}
and b_k, or an explicit factorization of F_lambda into restricted
Laguerre-Polya factors.
```

## Current Best Theorem Targets

Prove directly:

```text
h_k -> d_k(0)
```

is a Schur-positive specialization by giving positive parameters, a path model, or a production matrix.

Why best:

```text
It is exactly equivalent to the certified finite Toeplitz/PF evidence,
but asks for independent structure instead of extrapolation.
```

### Target 2: Edrei Log-Power Representation

Prove:

```text
log H_0(z) = gamma z + integral log(1 + beta z) dnu(beta)
```

with:

```text
gamma >= 0
nu positive on [0, infinity)
```

and sufficient convergence to reconstruct `H_0`.

Why best:

```text
It would explain both the log-sign diagnostics and the power-Hankel
necessary conditions, then supply the Edrei product form directly.
```

### Target 3: Determinantal Integral Formula

Prove every structurally nonzero Toeplitz minor has a positive integral representation.

Why best:

```text
It would bypass parameter reconstruction and prove PF-infinity directly.
```

Current status:

```text
No candidate formula with manifestly nonnegative integrand is known.
```

## Kill Gates

Reject a proposed theorem route if it:

```text
1. uses only finitely many coefficients;
2. assumes PF-infinity, Jensen hyperbolicity, LP membership, or RH;
3. proves only Hankel/Stieltjes positivity when Toeplitz PF is required;
4. merges direct kernel PF with coefficient PF;
5. uses asymptotic Jensen hyperbolicity as if it implied all-shift Jensen hyperbolicity;
6. treats a finite shifted-principal Hankel grid as all-order sign consistency;
7. depends only on local zero repulsion.
```

## Conclusion

No checked theorem currently closes the signed-Hankel/Jensen route.

The finite evidence is strong enough to make the following conjectural bridge precise:

```text
The normalized coefficient sequence d_k(0)=c_k(0)/c_0(0) is the complete
homogeneous specialization of a positive Edrei-Thoma/Schur-positive object.
```

But the missing proof is exactly the construction of that object, or an equivalent all-order determinant identity.
